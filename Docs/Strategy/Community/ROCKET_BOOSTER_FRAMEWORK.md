## üöÄ STAGE 1: IGNITION (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1: First Contact & Assessment

#### Day 1-3: Relationship Building
**Rocket Booster Actions:**
- Listen first, offer second
- Understand their existing strengths
- Map their current network and resources
- Identify their authentic goals (not what they think we want to hear)

**Key Questions:**
- "What would success look like if no one was watching?"
- "What resources do you already have that you might be overlooking?"
- "Who in your community has skills they haven't been asked to use?"

#### Day 4-7: Capacity Assessment
**Rocket Booster Tools:**
- Skills audit (who can do what)
- Network mapping (who knows who)
- Resource inventory (what's already available)
- Story collection (what successes already exist)

**Deliverable:** Community Capacity Map

### Week 2: Tool Transfer & Initial Training

#### Platform Access Setup
**What We Provide:**
- Intelligence platform access (6-month grant)
- Initial data connection (their existing systems)
- Basic training materials (self-paced)
- Support channel access (peer community)

**What We DON'T Provide:**
- Dependency-creating services
- Ongoing management
- Decision-making
- Problem-solving without teaching

#### First Success Sprint
**Goal:** Win something small in first 2 weeks
**Method:**
1. Identify easiest grant opportunity
2. Use platform to match existing stories
3. Apply together (them driving, us supporting)
4. Celebrate immediately when won

**Why:** Early success builds confidence and proves the tools work

### Week 3: Network Integration

#### Peer Connections
**Rocket Booster Actions:**
- Connect to similar communities (2-3 introductions)
- Add to appropriate peer learning circles
- Share relevant success patterns
- Make warm introductions to key contacts

**Community Actions:**
- Participate in peer calls
- Share their own challenges/successes
- Offer help to others
- Start building horizontal relationships

### Week 4: Independence Planning

#### Sustainability Discussion
**Key Conversation:**
"In 6 months, you won't need us. Let's plan for that from day one."

**Planning Elements:**
- Who will become the local expert?
- What skills need to be transferred?
- How will you support others in the network?
- What would 'graduation' look like?

**Tools Provided:**
- Independence checklist
- Skill transfer roadmap
- Peer mentor matching
- Success metrics (theirs, not ours)

## ‚ö° STAGE 2: THRUST (Months 2-3)

### Month 2: Supported Independence

#### Rocket Booster Role Shift
**From:** Doing for them
**To:** Teaching them to do

**Weekly Check-ins Become:**
- Problem-solving sessions (they present, we facilitate)
- Skill-building workshops (they practice, we guide)
- Peer introductions (they connect, we enable)
- Success celebrations (they achieve, we amplify)

#### Community Responsibilities Increase
**They Now Handle:**
- Platform usage without guidance
- First-level troubleshooting
- Peer support for newer communities
- Innovation and experimentation
- Local relationship building

### Month 3: Teaching Others

#### The Multiplication Moment
**Key Milestone:** Community starts teaching others

**How We Enable This:**
- Connect them to newer communities
- Provide teaching resources and templates
- Support their first mentoring relationships
- Document their innovations for sharing

**Why This Matters:**
When they teach, they own the knowledge completely

#### Innovation Encouragement
**Rocket Booster Role:**
- Celebrate experiments (successful or not)
- Share innovations across network
- Resource testing of new approaches
- Document learnings for others

**Community Innovations We've Seen:**
- Story circles (Rural Queensland)
- Grant writing sprints (Brisbane)
- Youth grant squads (Sydney)
- WhatsApp support bots (Rural/Remote)
- Cultural impact metrics (Indigenous)

## üö® Common Failure Modes

### How Rocket Boosters Fail

#### 1. The Dependency Trap
**Warning Signs:**
- Communities always calling for help
- No peer teaching happening
- Innovation stagnation
- Ongoing financial dependence

**Prevention:**
- Built-in obsolescence timeline
- Forced handover moments
- Peer mentor requirements
- Independence celebrations

#### 2. The Control Creep
**Warning Signs:**
- Wanting updates and reports
- Feeling need to guide decisions
- Concern about "brand dilution"
- Resistance to community innovations

**Prevention:**
- Regular self-assessment
- Community feedback on independence
- Celebration of divergent approaches
- Scheduled withdrawal checkpoints

#### 3. The Scale Seduction
**Warning Signs:**
- Focus on number of communities served
- Pride in geographic expansion
- Building permanent infrastructure
- Creating "ACT 2.0" territories

**Prevention:**
- Success metrics focused on independence
- Celebration of communities that don't need us
- Resource allocation toward obsolescence
- Regular philosophy reinforcement
