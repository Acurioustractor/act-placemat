## üåü Podcast Vision

**"The greatest organizations build movements that don't need them."**

The Obsolescence Movement is a weekly podcast celebrating communities that took flight after their rocket boost. Each episode features authentic voices of community members who found their own power, created their own solutions, and now teach others without any ongoing support from the organizations that initially helped them.

### Core Philosophy
- **Community voices first** - No host commentary or expert analysis
- **Authentic, unpolished stories** - Raw community conversations
- **Success through independence** - Celebrate communities that no longer need help
- **Innovation without permission** - Highlight community-created solutions
- **Beautiful obsolescence** - Honor organizations that worked themselves out of jobs

## üìä Podcast Structure

### Format
- **30-minute episodes** - Perfect for commutes and coffee breaks
- **Weekly release** - Every Tuesday at 6 AM Australian time

## üéôÔ∏è Production Framework

### Recording Approach
**Pre-Interview:**
- 30-minute conversation to build trust
- No questions about "problems" or "challenges"
- Focus on what they created and how it spread
- Understand their preferred communication style

**During Recording:**
- Start with celebration of their success
- Ask about their innovations, not our tools
- Let them tell the story their way
- Encourage storytelling, not reporting

**Post-Production:**
- Minimal editing - preserve authentic voice
- Add context subtly, never correct
- Create beautiful audio experience
- Generate accessible transcripts

### Key Interview Questions
1. "Tell us about the moment you realized you didn't need us anymore."
2. "What did you create that we never imagined?"
3. "How are you teaching others now?"
4. "What would you tell other communities just getting started?"
5. "What are you most proud of that has nothing to do with us?"
