## Cultural Framework

### Data Sovereignty Principles

1. **Indigenous Data Control**: Indigenous communities maintain authority over their cultural data
2. **Collective Benefit**: Data use must benefit the communities that create it
3. **Responsibility**: Platform acknowledges responsibility for cultural data stewardship
4. **Ethics**: All data practices align with Indigenous values and protocols

### Cultural Content Guidelines

1. **Sacred Knowledge Protection**: Sacred or restricted cultural information is protected
2. **Community Consent**: Communities provide informed consent for data sharing
3. **Elder Oversight**: Appropriate Elder review for cultural content
4. **Attribution**: Proper acknowledgment and credit for cultural contributions

### Community Governance

1. **Self-Determination**: Communities govern themselves according to their values
2. **Collaborative Decision-Making**: Platform decisions involve community input
3. **Transparent Processes**: Clear, open governance and appeal processes
4. **Cultural Advisory**: Elder Council and Cultural Advisors guide platform development

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Platform Questions

**Q: How is ACT Placemat different from other social platforms?**
A: ACT Placemat is specifically designed for community impact and cultural respect. We prioritise Indigenous data sovereignty, community benefit sharing, and meaningful social change over advertising revenue or user engagement metrics.

**Q: Is the platform free to use?**
A: Yes, the platform is free for community members. We sustain ourselves through partnership revenue and grants, with 40% of our value returning to communities.

**Q: How do you protect my privacy?**
A: We use industry-leading security measures, provide granular privacy controls, and comply with all relevant privacy legislation including Indigenous data sovereignty principles.

### Cultural Questions

**Q: How do you handle sensitive cultural content?**
A: We work with Elder Councils and Cultural Advisors to ensure appropriate protocols. Communities maintain control over their cultural data and can set specific sharing and access requirements.

**Q: What if I'm not Indigenous but want to share about Indigenous culture?**
A: We encourage learning and appreciation, but require appropriate consultation and permission from relevant communities. Our Cultural Advisors can help guide appropriate sharing practices.

**Q: How do I report culturally inappropriate content?**
A: Use the report function on any content, or contact our Cultural Advisory team directly at cultural@actplacemat.org.au for immediate attention.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What devices can I use to access the platform?**
A: ACT Placemat works on all modern web browsers, and we have native apps for iOS and Android devices. The platform is also accessible via assistive technologies.

**Q: Can I export my data?**
A: Yes, you have full rights to export your data at any time through your account settings. We support standard data formats for easy migration.

**Q: How do I report technical issues?**
A: Contact our support team at support@actplacemat.org.au or use the help function within the platform. Include as much detail as possible about the issue you're experiencing.
