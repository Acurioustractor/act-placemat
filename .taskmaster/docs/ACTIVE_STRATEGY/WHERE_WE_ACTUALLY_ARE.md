## Embedding Learning and Failure

### Why This Matters
We're trying to build something NEW - there's no playbook for "community-owned innovation cooperative inspired by Indigenous values while being respectful non-Indigenous allies." We WILL make mistakes. Let's plan for it.

### Learning Practices to Build In

**1. Weekly Reflections**
- Every Friday: What did we learn? What failed? What's working?
- Document in shared space
- No blame, just curiosity

**2. Monthly Retrospectives**
- What experiments did we run?
- What results did we get?
- What do we do differently next month?

**3. Quarterly Community Reviews**
- Share what we tried and what we learned
- Ask: What should we stop/start/continue?
- Let community shape direction

**4. Annual Values Audit**
- Are we living our values or just talking about them?
- Cultural checkpoint: Are we walking our talk with Indigenous engagement?
- Adjust course based on honest assessment

### Permission to Fail

**Things we might fail at (and that's OK)**:
- First governance experiments might be clunky
- Some projects will need to be killed
- R&D claims might be smaller than hoped
- Community engagement might be slow to build
- We'll make cultural missteps (apologize, learn, do better)

**How we handle failure**:
1. **Notice**: Regular reflection catches failure early
2. **Name**: Be honest about what's not working
3. **Learn**: What does this teach us?
4. **Adjust**: Change course based on learning
5. **Share**: Tell the community what we learned

**Not OK to fail at**:
- Financial sustainability (burning through $500K carelessly)
- Cultural respect (appropriating without accountability)
- Community trust (promising what we don't deliver)
- Safety (physical, psychological, cultural)
