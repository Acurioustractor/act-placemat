# ACT Community Benefit-Sharing Legal Framework
## Revolutionary 40% Profit Guarantee and Community Ownership Model

*Philosophy: "Communities create value, communities own value"*  
*Embodies: Economic Justice, Legal Certainty, Community Sovereignty*

---

## Executive Summary

This legal framework establishes ACT's revolutionary commitment to community benefit-sharing, legally guaranteeing that **40% of all profits** generated from community participation, stories, innovations, or data will be returned to those communities. This represents the first legally-binding community profit-sharing model in the social enterprise sector.

## Legal Structure

### 1. Corporate Governance Framework

**ACT Placemat Pty Ltd** operates under a hybrid corporate structure combining:

- **Traditional Corporate Structure** (60% revenue retention for platform sustainability)
- **Community Benefit Corporation Model** (40% community profit-sharing obligation)
- **Indigenous Economic Sovereignty Principles** (where applicable)
- **Cooperative Governance Elements** (community decision-making rights)

### 2. Community Benefit-Sharing Charter

#### Article I: Fundamental Guarantee
> "ACT Placemat Pty Ltd hereby irrevocably commits to distribute no less than **forty percent (40%)** of net profits to communities whose participation, stories, innovations, cultural knowledge, or data contributed to the generation of said profits."

#### Article II: Legal Enforceability
- This commitment is legally binding under Australian corporate law
- Communities have standing to enforce benefit-sharing obligations
- Independent auditing ensures compliance and transparency
- Community benefit-sharing takes precedence over executive compensation

#### Article III: Community Ownership Rights
Communities maintain:
- **Data Ownership**: Complete ownership of their stories and cultural knowledge
- **Profit Participation**: Legal right to 40% of profits generated from their contributions
- **Governance Participation**: Voting rights on benefit-sharing distribution methods
- **Cultural Protocol Respect**: Legal protection of cultural decision-making processes

## Value Generation and Attribution Framework

### 1. Value Source Classification

**Primary Value Sources** (eligible for benefit-sharing):
- **Community Stories**: Personal and cultural narratives shared by community members
- **Cultural Knowledge**: Traditional knowledge, protocols, and wisdom
- **Innovation and Ideas**: Community-generated solutions and innovations
- **Participation and Engagement**: Active community participation that creates platform value
- **Data and Insights**: Community-generated data that creates business value
- **Advocacy Impact**: Policy changes or social impact resulting from community stories

**Platform Value Sources** (retained for sustainability):
- **Technology Infrastructure**: Platform development and maintenance
- **Marketing and Outreach**: Platform promotion and user acquisition
- **Administrative Services**: Legal, accounting, and operational services
- **Risk Management**: Insurance, compliance, and business continuity

### 2. Attribution Methodology

#### Transparent Value Tracking
All value generation is tracked using:
- **Blockchain-based ledger** for immutable transaction records
- **AI-powered attribution algorithms** that respect privacy while tracking contribution
- **Community-verified metrics** ensuring fair attribution
- **Independent auditing** by certified public accountants

#### Attribution Principles
1. **Direct Attribution**: Clear connection between community contribution and value generated
2. **Proportional Distribution**: Benefits distributed based on relative contribution levels
3. **Community Input**: Communities participate in defining attribution methodologies
4. **Cultural Respect**: Attribution methods respect Indigenous and cultural protocols
5. **Privacy Protection**: Individual privacy maintained while enabling community benefit

## Legal Distribution Mechanisms

### 1. Profit Distribution Structure

**Total Net Profit Allocation:**
- **40% Community Benefit-Sharing** (legally guaranteed minimum)
- **20% Platform Sustainability** (infrastructure, development, operations)
- **15% Innovation Fund** (community-driven innovation projects)
- **10% Reserve Fund** (emergency and long-term sustainability)
- **10% Impact Expansion** (supporting additional communities)
- **5% Executive and Staff** (performance-based, capped compensation)

### 2. Community Distribution Methods

- **Story Contributors**: Direct payments to storytellers based on story usage and impact
- **Cultural Knowledge Holders**: Compensation for traditional knowledge sharing

### 3. Payment Mechanisms
